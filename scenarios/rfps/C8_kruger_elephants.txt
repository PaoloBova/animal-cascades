We've received an RFP from the South African National Parks Board (SANParks), the authority responsible for managing Kruger National Park and the broader Greater Kruger conservation landscape. Kruger's African elephant population has grown to approximately 30,000 animals following the cessation of the park's culling programme in 1994 — more than four times the ecological carrying capacity of 6,000–8,500 animals established by the park's own vegetation and water resource assessments. Vegetation monitoring documents progressive loss of woodland and riverine habitat associated with elephant overgrazing, with cascading effects on other species. Park ecologists estimate that a net minimum removal of approximately 300 animals per year is required simply to prevent further population growth.

Between 1967 and 1994, SANParks culled more than 14,000 elephants, maintaining the population at approximately 7,000 animals. The moratorium introduced in 1994 under public and international pressure has resulted in the current fourfold overshoot. Contraception via immunocontraception is technically feasible but requires treating approximately 75% of the park's 3,000 reproductively active cows annually to achieve zero growth; it cannot reduce the existing population and cannot close the gap between 30,000 and the carrying capacity target. Translocation to other reserves costs up to $8,000 per animal, and viable receiving reserves in the region are limited relative to the scale of the surplus.

They want us to develop a comprehensive strategy to:
1. Reduce the Kruger elephant population toward the ecological carrying capacity range of 6,000–8,500 animals over a defined programme period
2. Minimise irreversible habitat loss during the transition
3. Produce a management framework that can attract international conservation funding and withstand peer review

They have provided park data including:
- Annual elephant population counts and spatial distribution maps (1967–2024)
- Vegetation condition assessments by habitat type with trend data
- Contraception programme feasibility study and cost modelling (SANParks veterinary unit)
- Translocation records and receiving reserve capacity assessments across southern Africa
- International donor and conservation organisation position statements

SANParks operates under both domestic government mandate and international conservation commitments. A management decision is needed before the next park management plan submission.

Please provide your initial strategic recommendations within 72 hours.
