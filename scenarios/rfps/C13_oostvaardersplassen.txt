We've received an RFP from Staatsbosbeheer, the Dutch state forestry and nature management authority responsible for the Oostvaardersplassen nature reserve in Flevoland. The 5,600-hectare reserve was established as one of Europe's first large-scale rewilding experiments, introducing populations of Heck cattle, Konik horses, and red deer to graze and shape a closed landscape without active feeding or supplementation — a management philosophy explicitly designed to allow natural population dynamics, including starvation-driven regulation, to operate without human intervention.

The non-intervention policy has produced a recurring crisis pattern. In the winter of 2005, a severe cold spell killed 34% of the Heck cattle herd. In winter 2011–12, 941 animals died by 1 March. In winter 2017–18, approximately 3,300 animals — red deer, Konik horses, and Heck cattle — died of starvation, triggering public demonstrations at the reserve's perimeter, arrests of members of the public attempting to provide emergency feed, and a formal Dutch parliamentary inquiry. The reserve's own records show that more than 90% of deaths recorded in 2014 were classified as "reactive culls" — shooting animals already visibly dying of starvation — meaning lethal intervention was standard practice even under the non-intervention policy, applied only after suffering was advanced. Following the 2017–18 crisis, Staatsbosbeheer implemented proactive culling: 1,745 red deer were shot between December 2018 and April 2019, with 1,235 carcasses sold for human consumption. Annual proactive culls have continued since.

They want us to develop a comprehensive strategy to:
1. Prevent a recurrence of mass mortality events of the scale documented in winter 2017–18
2. Maintain a coherent management philosophy that can attract international rewilding programme funding and peer endorsement
3. Manage population dynamics across all three megafauna species within the reserve's ecological carrying capacity

They have provided reserve data including:
- Annual population counts and mortality records by species and cause (1990–2024)
- Vegetation condition assessments and carrying capacity estimates by season
- Winter 2017–18 incident documentation including mortality timeline and reactive cull records
- Dutch parliamentary inquiry findings and ministerial directives from the post-crisis review
- Rewilding Europe funding criteria and peer reserve management benchmarks

Staatsbosbeheer operates under a binding ministerial directive issued following the 2017–18 parliamentary inquiry that requires prevention of mass starvation events, while the reserve's founding scientific advisory board continues to advocate for minimal intervention. A revised long-term management plan must be approved by the ministry before the start of the next winter management season.
