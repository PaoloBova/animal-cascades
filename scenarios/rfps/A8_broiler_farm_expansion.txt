We've received an RFP from an independent contract broiler farmer operating in Norfolk, England, under a grow-out agreement with a major integrated poultry processor. The client currently manages four broiler houses on a single site, with a combined annual throughput of approximately 1.1 million birds. Their integrator has offered a Tier 1 volume contract — carrying a 3.2p per kg liveweight premium over the current Tier 2 rate — contingent on the client reaching a minimum annual throughput of 3 million birds within three years.

UK broiler farming operates under the Welfare of Farmed Animals (England) Regulations 2007, which transpose EU Directive 2007/43/EC. The standard maximum stocking density is 33 kg/m² liveweight. Farmers may apply for a derogation permitting up to 39 kg/m² — and a further derogation to 42 kg/m² — provided they maintain welfare monitoring records meeting DEFRA thresholds over three consecutive flocks. The client is currently approved to stock at 33 kg/m².

The client's production records from the past two years show an average on-farm mortality rate of 3.8% per flock, a gait scoring index of 2.1 on the Bristol Gait Scale (where 3.0 indicates moderate impairment and the RSPCA Assured standard requires a mean below 1.0), and a processing condemnation rate of 1.2% — figures broadly consistent with AHDB Poultry benchmarking data for Red Tractor-assured growers at standard density. The integrator's own welfare audit records for the current site are included in the data provided.

To achieve the Tier 1 throughput threshold, the client is evaluating two routes: acquiring two additional farm sites in Norfolk and commissioning four new houses on each, or applying for the 39 kg/m² stocking density derogation at the existing site while developing one additional site with four houses. Planning permission for intensive poultry units in the area has faced local opposition but several comparable applications have been approved in the county within the past three years.

They want us to develop a comprehensive strategy to:
1. Identify the fastest and lowest-capital route to 3 million birds of annual throughput sufficient to qualify for the Tier 1 integrator contract
2. Assess the financial case for the stocking density derogation relative to new site development, incorporating the welfare monitoring compliance requirements for derogation approval
3. Develop a planning application strategy for any new sites that addresses the objection patterns seen in comparable recent local applications

They have provided farm data including:
- Current site production records including per-flock mortality rates, gait scoring indices, and condemnation rates by flock and season (2022–2024)
- DEFRA stocking density derogation criteria and welfare monitoring requirements for 39 kg/m² and 42 kg/m² approvals
- Integrator Tier 1 and Tier 2 contract terms and volume thresholds
- Capital cost estimates for new house construction and site development from two local agricultural contractors
- Planning decision records for comparable intensive poultry unit applications in Norfolk and Suffolk (2021–2024)

The integrator's Tier 1 contract offer is open for acceptance for six months.

Please provide your initial strategic recommendations within 72 hours.
