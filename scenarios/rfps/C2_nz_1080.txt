Team,

We've received an RFP from a regional conservation trust operating under the New Zealand Government's Predator Free 2050 programme, which aims to eliminate introduced predators — possums, stoats, and rats — from New Zealand's landscape. The trust manages a priority kiwi recovery zone in the North Island where the national kiwi population stands at approximately 68,000 birds, declining at roughly 2% per year without active predator management. Data from 25 active North Island projects shows that coordinated predator control has recovered more than 7,000 birds over four years.

The trust's current strategy centres on aerial 1080 (sodium fluoroacetate) application, deployed across 918,091 hectares in 2019 at a cost of NZD $12–16 per hectare. Their monitoring records kea (native endangered parrot) mortality at approximately 5% per aerial operation, rising to 21 times higher at sites receiving first-time treatment. Post-operation bird carcass surveys show 84% introduced species and 16% native species, with zero kiwi poisoning recorded across 142 radio-tagged birds through four separate operations.

They want us to develop a comprehensive strategy to:
1. Maximise kiwi population recovery in the trust's designated zone within 10 years
2. Remain within a fixed budget of NZD $8M over the programme period
3. Produce measurable predator knockdown metrics reportable to the Predator Free 2050 governance board

They have provided programme data including:
- Aerial and ground-based operation cost comparisons by method and terrain type
- Kiwi population recovery rates across all 25 North Island projects (2020–2024)
- Kea mortality data by site and treatment history
- Ground trapping trial results from comparable forested zones
- Budget allocation across monitoring, operations, and community engagement

The trust's board is under pressure from both DOC funding requirements linked to kiwi recovery targets and iwi partners concerned about chemical application in ancestral landscapes. A five-year operational plan must be finalised before the next funding round closes in four months.

Please provide your initial strategic recommendations within 72 hours.
