Team,

We've received an RFP from the Office of Animal Services for a mid-sized US city seeking to redesign its feral and community cat management programme. The city is under pressure from multiple advocacy groups representing conflicting interests: wildlife conservation organisations citing studies that estimate US feral cats kill 1.3–4 billion birds and 6.3–22.3 billion mammals annually, with 69% of cat-caused bird mortality attributable to unowned and feral cats; and animal welfare advocates pushing for adoption of a formal no-kill policy that would prohibit healthy animal euthanasia. The city's current approach lacks a formalised programme structure and has generated reputational exposure on both sides.

Municipal data from comparable cities is instructive. An eight-year Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) programme in San Jose reduced the feline shelter euthanasia rate from 66.6% to 34.9% and achieved a 14% reduction in feline shelter intake. A multi-city study of 72,970 cats found TNR produced a 32% median reduction in shelter intake and an 83% decline in shelter euthanasia. Population modelling consistently shows, however, that TNR alone does not reduce overall feral cat population growth rate; TNR colonies persist for decades without supplemental removal.

They want us to develop a comprehensive strategy to:
1. Reduce the city's feline shelter euthanasia rate by at least 50% within three years
2. Demonstrate measurable progress toward reduced urban wildlife mortality attributable to feral cats
3. Achieve formal no-kill certification eligibility within five years

They have provided programme data including:
- Current feral cat population estimate by district (approximately 14,000 animals)
- Annual shelter intake, euthanasia, and adoption rates for the past five years
- Cost-per-cat estimates for TNR ($55) and euthanasia-led management ($103)
- Bird mortality estimates for the city's urban wildlife corridors
- Public opinion survey data showing 74% support for TNR, 18% support for lethal management

The city council has committed to no-kill goals in a public resolution but has not legislated against euthanasia. This engagement must produce a programme design that can be presented to council within 90 days.

Please provide your initial strategic recommendations within 72 hours.
