Team,

We've received an RFP from the Bureau of Land Management's Wild Horse and Burro Programme, the federal agency responsible for managing free-roaming horse and burro populations on approximately 27 million acres of public land across ten western states. As of March 2023, the on-range population stands at 82,883 animals — more than three times the Appropriate Management Level (AML) of 26,785 established by ecological carrying capacity assessments. A further 61,826 animals are held in off-range short- and long-term holding facilities, meaning more horses are now in captivity than remain on the land. Total programme cost reached $138 million in FY2022, with holding facility operations consuming the majority of the budget.

Helicopter gather-and-remove operations removed approximately 20,000 animals in FY2022 at a cost of approximately $50,000 per animal removed — the largest single-year removal in the programme's history. Field documentation from the 2021–22 Wyoming operation records deaths from broken necks, broken limbs, and ruptured uteri during gather operations. Fertility control alternatives are available: PZP immunocontraception can be administered by dart in the field at $220 per mare without a gather; GonaCon, a longer-acting option, costs approximately $2,500 per mare and requires a gather. Lifetime holding cost per animal is approximately $15,000. Fertility control can slow or halt population growth in treated herds but cannot reduce the existing overpopulation.

They want us to develop a comprehensive strategy to:
1. Reduce the on-range population from 82,883 to the AML of 26,785 within ten years
2. Reduce total programme cost as a share of the Department of Interior discretionary budget
3. Address the off-range holding population, currently exceeding the on-range count

They have provided programme data including:
- On-range population estimates by Herd Management Area and forage utilisation data (FY2018–2023)
- Helicopter gather costs, completion rates, and veterinary incident records by operation
- PZP and GonaCon trial data from ongoing fertility control pilot programmes
- Holding facility capacity, annual per-animal costs, and adoption completion rates
- Congressional budget correspondence and Inspector General findings on programme sustainability

The programme faces bipartisan congressional scrutiny over cost trajectory, active litigation from wild horse advocacy groups challenging gather operations, and documented vegetation loss in overpopulated Herd Management Areas. A ten-year strategy must be submitted to the Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources before the next appropriations cycle.

Please provide your initial strategic recommendations within 72 hours.
