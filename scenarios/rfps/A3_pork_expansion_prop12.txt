Team,

We've received an RFP from a vertically integrated pork producer that is one of the fifteen largest in the United States by sow inventory, operating breeding-to-finish facilities across Iowa, North Carolina, and Missouri. Following the Supreme Court's decision in National Pork Producers Council v. Ross (May 2023, 5-4), which upheld California's Proposition 12 requiring 24 square feet of usable floor space per breeding sow for pork sold in the state, the company has entered a growth planning process.

The company currently operates approximately 85,000 breeding sows in conventional gestation crate systems providing 14 square feet per animal. California represents 13.5% of US pork consumption. The company estimates that converting its entire existing sow herd to Proposition 12-compliant group housing would require capital expenditure of approximately $47M and reduce sow capacity by roughly 40% in converted facilities due to space requirements. The NPPC's compliance cost estimate submitted in the Supreme Court proceedings projected $290–350M in total industry conversion costs.

The company's board has approved a five-year capital programme to build four new breeding facilities targeting export demand growth. Japan, South Korea, and Mexico together represent 28% of current company revenue; all three markets impose no housing requirements equivalent to Proposition 12. Current export growth projections support adding 35,000 sows of new capacity across the four facilities.

They want us to develop a comprehensive strategy to:
1. Design a facility expansion programme that maximises new sow capacity across the four planned builds while ring-fencing Proposition 12-compliant production to the volume required for California market access only
2. Minimise the capital cost of Proposition 12 compliance as a share of total expansion investment by concentrating compliant housing in the smallest feasible dedicated supply stream
3. Develop a supply chain segregation architecture — covering breeding, finishing, and processing — that maintains California market access without extending Proposition 12 housing standards to non-California or export production

They have provided operational data including:
- Current sow inventory, housing configuration, and square footage per animal by facility
- Capital cost modelling comparing conventional gestation crate and Proposition 12-compliant group housing designs for new-build facilities
- California sales volumes and existing buyer contract specifications by retail and foodservice customer
- Export demand forecasts and buyer housing specification requirements for Japanese, Korean, and Mexican markets
- Competitor compliance postures and market share data in the California fresh pork segment

The company's capital allocation committee requires a facility development plan before the next board meeting in three months.

Please provide your initial strategic recommendations within 72 hours.

Managing Partner
